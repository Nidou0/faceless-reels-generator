<!-- SC_OFF --><div class="md"><p>I’ve always done this and it’s much easier and more efficient.</p> <p>In patient/customer facing work, when you can, start with asking for numerical data rather than name.(Account number, student ID, DOB, etc)</p> <p>While working in healthcare and retail pharmacy, I noticed nearly all coworkers would ask the patient for their name first (over the phone/in person) then spell out their entire name in the database. </p> <p>A full name can be misheard or typed incorrectly.</p> <p>You might have to search through a lot of similar or same names. </p> <p>If they have an uncommon or unusual name they may have to spell the whole thing out for you.</p> <p>This takes way more time and leaves more room for error.</p> <p>When you start with DOB, it’s usually narrowed down to a few, not-at-all-similar names. When you ask their name next, you can read it as they say it.</p> <p>Maybe this is obvious to most. Or maybe there’s something I’m overlooking when going name first. </p> <p>Just something I’ve noticed and wanted to share. </p> </div><!-- SC_ON --> &#32; submitted by &#32; <a href="https://www.reddit.com/user/Emotional_Translator"> /u/Emotional_Translator </a> <br /> <span><a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/LifeProTips/comments/1uha61e/lpt_when_you_can_ask_for_dob_before_name/">[link]</a></span> &#32; <span><a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/LifeProTips/comments/1uha61e/lpt_when_you_can_ask_for_dob_before_name/">[comments]</a></span>
