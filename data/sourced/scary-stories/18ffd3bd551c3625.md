One bedroom, one bathroom, a narrow kitchen, and a living room with windows that look out over the back alley. I know every crack in the paint, every sound the pipes make at night, every place where the floorboards complain when you step on them.</p> <p>That is why I noticed the door immediately.</p> <p>It was in the hallway between my bedroom and the bathroom, where there had always been a blank wall.</p> <p>A white wooden door.</p> <p>Plain. Old. Perfectly ordinary.</p> <p>Except it had not been there the night before.</p> <p>At first, I thought I was still dreaming. I stood in the hallway in my T-shirt and sweatpants, holding a mug of coffee I could not remember pouring, staring at the brass doorknob.</p> <p>There was no frame damage. No dust. No smell of fresh paint. It looked like it had always belonged there.</p> <p>I touched the wall around it.</p> <p>Solid.</p> <p>I touched the door.</p> <p>Cold.</p> <p>Not cool like wood in the morning, but cold like something kept underground.</p> <p>I called my landlord.</p> <p>“Very funny,” he said.</p> <p>“I’m not joking.”</p> <p>There was a pause.</p> <p>“What kind of door?”</p> <p>“What do you mean, what kind of door? Not desperate.</p> <p>Polite.</p> <p>Like someone waiting to be invited in.</p> <p>I backed away into the living room and grabbed the heaviest thing I could find: a cast iron pan from the kitchen.
